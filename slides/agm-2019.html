What is our purpose?

Let's start with our
Community Interest statement

Submitted to Companies House in January 2015

Activity 1

Providing programmes of study for the general public

"To help develop the knowledge and skills of the general population."

Activity 2

Providing a place of learning and work for our members

"To provide a convivial place where our members can study and work together and contribute to our study programmes; to encourage continuing support and collaboration between students of our study programmes and our members."

"Our activities are run and managed by our members, most of whom have been through one of our study programmes and are committed through their membership of the Company to continue to contribute to those programmes."

From the very beginning, the idea was to create a peer-led environment, not just for learning, but also for work.

Vision and mission statements

Adopted at our 2017 AGM, just as we were opening the Nazareth campus

Vision

"to be an open-source model that enables anyone anywhere to establish a democratically-organised learning community that is free at the point of access."

Mission

"to open access to education by building democratically-organised learning communities where people study creative technology."

The scope of the original community interest statement was both wider and narrower. Wider, because it encompasses, not just learning, but work. Narrower, because it is only about a single place.

Where are we now?

  • We are paying more attention to the London programme
  • Through Tech for Better, we are creating more social impact
  • Through the Founders programme, we are growing our developer community
  • Through our introductory courses, we are expanding our engagement with the general public
